Now for Paragraph Two I will discuss the absurdity that is the Youtube ad policy. I, as most other users I'm sure, use Ad block on my browsers as I am absolutely not going to wait for a 1-5 minute ad to play so I can watch a quick video on how to cook a chicken. The issue now arises that Youtubes response to this is to attempt to punish those who use adblockers by not allowing them to actually watch Youtube videos, and to punish those who don't use adblockers by forcing even more ads onto them to make up the difference. This of course, only works because there has yet to be any true competition to Youtube in terms of video sharing and audience. The most effective solution would be for a competitor to provide a platform of similar quality, which will be difficult as Youtube has decades of QA experience at this point, and at a smaller cost, which would also be difficult as servers are costly and require individuals to maintain them. Truly an unfortunate situation. That is my second paragraph of text.
